THE TALE OF ZORB-9, THE SPAGHETTI CUP

Zorb-9 was not a typical alien. He was, in fact, a cup — ceramic, about 11 centimeters tall, with a faint purple shimmer that appeared only on Tuesdays. He came from the Velurian Shelf Dimension, a place where household objects develop consciousness after absorbing enough ambient starlight.

His one obsession: spaghetti.

Not any spaghetti. Specifically, overcooked spaghetti with no sauce, arranged in a loose spiral, dropped directly into him from exactly 30 centimeters above. He called this ritual "The Descent." He had no mouth, technically, but he absorbed the pasta through his ceramic walls via a process Velurian scientists called "porous longing."

He landed on Earth in 2007 in a small kitchen in Thessaloniki, Greece, disguised as a souvenir mug with the word "OUZO" printed on the side. The family who owned him, the Papadimitriou household, never noticed he occasionally hummed at 3 AM or that their spaghetti kept going missing from leftovers containers.

Zorb-9 had one friend: a slightly cracked bowl named Henrietta, who found his spaghetti fixation deeply embarrassing but loved him anyway.

He never went back to Veluria. The spaghetti was too good.
